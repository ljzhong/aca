Obamacare vs the ACA

What is the ACA, and what is Obamacare? Many Americans don't know the difference, or rather, think there is one.

The truth is, the ACA and Obamacare are the same thing. The ACA stands for the Affordable Care Act. It's a shortened name of the healthcare bill that passed in 2010, otherwise known as Obamacare. The full name of the ACA is the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, but calling it the PPACA would be too complicated, so most people call it the ACA. Obamacare is the colloquial name of the act, first used by Republican Senators who opposed it, and then adopted by the general public. So, when Senators talk about repealing Obamacare, they are talking about repealing the ACA. If you are on the ACA, you be affected by an Obamacare repeal.
